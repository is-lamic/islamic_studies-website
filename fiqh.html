What is Fiqh?

Fiqh refers to Islamic jurisprudence, the understanding and practice of Islamic laws derived from the Quran and Sunnah. It covers all aspects of life, including worship, transactions, marriage, and criminal law.

Schools of Fiqh

  • Hanafi: Founded by Imam Abu Hanifa, emphasizing reason and analogy.
  • Maliki: Founded by Imam Malik, relying heavily on the practices of the people of Madinah.
  • Shafi'i: Founded by Imam Shafi'i, combining Quran, Hadith, consensus, and analogy.
  • Hanbali: Founded by Imam Ahmad ibn Hanbal, focusing strictly on the Quran and Hadith.
